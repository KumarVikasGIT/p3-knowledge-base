# Gallery access levels: Viewer, Editor, Owner

When you invite someone to a gallery, you'll choose one of two roles. A third role — Owner — exists but isn't something you assign; it belongs automatically to whoever created the gallery.

## Owner

Whoever creates a gallery is its Owner. Every gallery has exactly one. The Owner can do everything: upload, caption, delete any photo, invite and remove collaborators, edit gallery settings, create and revoke share links, delete the gallery entirely, and transfer ownership to someone else.

Ownership isn't something you invite someone into — it can only move from one person to another via an explicit transfer.

## Editor

Can view, download, upload new photos, add or edit captions, and delete photos *they personally uploaded*. Cannot delete other people's photos, invite others, change gallery settings, or create share links.

This is the right role for someone actively contributing to a gallery — a second photographer on a shoot, for example.

## Viewer

Can view, download, and favourite photos. Cannot upload, caption, or delete anything, and cannot invite others or change settings.

This is the right role for someone who should simply see and download the photos — most friends, family, or general collaborators fall here.

## Changing someone's role later

You don't need to remove and re-invite someone to change their role — tap their role badge in the collaborators panel and select the new one. This takes effect immediately and doesn't require a new key exchange, since the gallery's encryption key doesn't change when a role changes (only when access is removed entirely — see [Removing someone from a gallery](./3-7-remove-collaborator.md)).
