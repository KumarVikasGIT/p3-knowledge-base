# What are my options if I lose access?

If you can't get into your P3 account or your galleries are showing as locked, your options depend on what you still have. Work through these in order.

## Do you remember your account password?

If you can still log in but your photos won't decrypt — for example, on a new device — your password isn't the issue. Skip ahead to the next question.

If you genuinely can't log in, see [I forgot my account password](./forgot-password.md). This gets you back into your account, but on its own it does not restore access to your photos — those are protected separately. Keep reading after resetting your password.

## Do you have your recovery phrase?

This is the 12-word phrase you were shown once, during key setup. If you have it — written down, in a password manager, anywhere safe — go to [Recovering with your recovery phrase](./recover-with-mnemonic.md). This is the primary, complete way back into your encrypted galleries.

## Did you set up a backup password?

If you don't have your phrase, but you remember setting up a backup password in Settings → Security at some point, see [Recovering with a backup password](./recover-with-backup-password.md). This is a separate, independent path that works even without the phrase.

## If you have neither

If you don't have your recovery phrase and never set a backup password, see [I lost my recovery phrase and have no backup password](./lost-mnemonic-no-backup.md). It's worth reading in full before deciding anything — it explains exactly what your options are at that point, honestly and without sugarcoating.

## Quick summary

|---|---|
