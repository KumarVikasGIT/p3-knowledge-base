# What works when I'm offline?

P3 keeps a local copy of your recently viewed content, so a fair amount still works without an internet connection — though not everything.

## What works offline

- **Viewing galleries and photos you've already opened.** Since photos are decrypted and cached locally on your device after the first view, you can browse them again without a connection.
- **Browsing your gallery list**, since basic gallery information is also stored locally and syncs in the background when you're online.

## What doesn't work offline

- **Uploading new photos.** Uploads will queue locally and wait — they're not lost, but they won't actually transfer until your connection returns. You'll see a clear indicator that you're offline and uploads are paused.
- **Viewing a gallery for the first time**, or any photo you haven't already opened on this device — since that requires downloading and decrypting it, which needs a connection.
- **Inviting collaborators, creating share links, or any action involving the server.**

## What happens when you reconnect

P3 detects the connection automatically and resumes anything that was waiting — queued uploads pick back up, and your gallery list refreshes with anything that changed while you were offline. You don't need to manually retry anything in most cases.

## If you're planning to be offline for a while

If you know you'll need a specific gallery while disconnected — traveling, for example — it's worth opening and browsing through it once while you still have a connection, so it's cached locally and ready when you're not.
